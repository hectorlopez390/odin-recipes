Chilaquiles

Description

Chilaquiles are a traditional Mexican breakfast dish consisting of corn tortillas cut into smaller pieces and lightly fried. Typically, corn tortillas cut into quarters and lightly fried or baked for a lighter version are the basis of the dish. Green or red salsa is poured over the crips tortilla triangles. Chilaquiles can have different variations throughout different regions in Mexico and can also have family variations. The dish is commonly garnished with crema, crumbled queso fresco, sliced onion, and avocado slices. Usually, chilaquiles are eaten at breakfast or runch. This makes them a popular recipe to use leftover tortillas and salsas.

Ingredients

  • 12 corn tortillas, preferably stale, or left out overnight to dry out a bit, quartered or cut into 6 wedges
  • Corn oil, or other neutral oil for frying
  • Salt
  • 1 1/2 to 2 cups red chile sauce or salsa verde
  • Springs of epazote

    • Garnishes

  • Cotija cheese or queso fresco
  • Crema Mexicana or creme fraiche
  • 1 red onion, chopped
  • Avocado, sliced or roughly chopped

Steps

  1. In a large saute pan, coat pan generously with corn oil, (1/8 inch), head on medium to high
  2. When the oil is quite hot, add the tortillas, fry until golden brown
  3. Remove tortillas to a paper towel lined plate soak up excess oil
  4. Sprinkle a little salt on the tortillas
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of oil to pan, bring to high heat again
  6. Add the salsa and let salsa cook for several minutes.
  7. Add a few sprigs of epazote to the salsa
  8. Add the fried tortilla quarters to the salsa
  9. Gently turn over the pieces of tortilla until they are well coated with salsa and let cook for a few minutes more
  10. Remove from heat, serve chilaquiles with garnishes,fried eggs, beans or nopalitos and enjoy!
